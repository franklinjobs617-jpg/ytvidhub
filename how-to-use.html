How to Use Our Downloader

This comprehensive guide walks you through every feature of YTVidHub, from downloading a single subtitle file to using our powerful bulk tool.

Guide: Downloading for a Single Video

This is the most common use case. Follow these simple steps to get the subtitles for any public YouTube video in seconds.

  1. Find Your YouTube Video: Open YouTube in your browser and navigate to the video whose subtitles you want to download.
  2. Copy the Video URL: Select the full URL from your browser's address bar and copy it to your clipboard. It should look something like `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=...`.
  3. Paste the URL into YTVidHub: Return to the YTVidHub homepage and paste the copied URL into the main input box.
  4. Click "Download Subtitles": Press the main download button. Our system will immediately fetch the available subtitles for that video. You'll be presented with options to download in SRT or TXT format.

Advanced: Using the Bulk Downloader

For power users, our bulk feature is a massive time-saver. It's perfect for downloading subtitles from entire playlists, online courses, or a collection of research videos.

  • Prepare Your List of URLs: In a text editor, create a list of all the YouTube URLs you need. Place each URL on a new line.
  • Paste the Entire List: Copy your complete list and paste it into the YTVidHub input box. Our tool is designed to recognize and process each line as a separate video.
  • Download Your ZIP Archive: Click the download button. The tool will work through your list and bundle all the subtitle files into a single, organized ZIP file. The files inside will be named after their video titles, so you can easily identify them.

Understanding Formats & Features

What is an SRT File?

SRT (SubRip Subtitle) is the most common subtitle file format. It's a plain-text file that contains not just the subtitle text, but also the precise start and end timestamps. This is the format you need if you want to import subtitles into video players like VLC or video editing software like Adobe Premiere Pro.

What is a TXT File?

A TXT file is a simple, plain-text version of the subtitles, also known as a transcript. It contains all the spoken words from the video without any timing information. This format is perfect if you need the text for a blog post, for study notes, or for data analysis.

Troubleshooting & Common Questions

Why does the tool say 'Subtitles not found'?

This message appears if the YouTube video you entered either does not have captions enabled or has them set to private. Our tool can only access publicly available subtitles (both auto-generated and manually created).

The download isn't starting. What should I do?

First, check your internet connection. Second, try disabling any ad-blockers or script-blocking browser extensions, as they can sometimes interfere with the download process. If the problem persists, try a different browser.

What's the difference between auto-generated and manual subtitles?

Auto-generated subtitles are created by YouTube's speech recognition technology and can sometimes contain inaccuracies in punctuation and wording. Manual subtitles are uploaded by the video creator and are typically much more accurate. Our tool downloads whichever version is available for the video, prioritizing manual subtitles if they exist.
