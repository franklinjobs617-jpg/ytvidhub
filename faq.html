General Questions

Is YTVidHub completely free to use?

Yes, our tool is 100% free. There are no daily limits, subscription fees, or hidden charges. Our goal is to provide a powerful and accessible tool for everyone.

Do I need to create an account to use the service?

No registration is required. YTVidHub is designed to be as simple as possible. Just visit our site, paste your URL(s), and download your files instantly.

Is this service legal?

Downloading publicly available subtitles for personal use, such as for accessibility, language learning, or research, generally falls under fair use. However, you should always respect the copyright of the content creator. Do not reuse subtitles in your own commercial work without permission.

Using the Tool

How do I download subtitles for a single video?

Simply copy the URL of the YouTube video you're interested in, paste it into the input box on our homepage, and click the 'Download Subtitles' button. You'll then be given the option to save the subtitles as an SRT or TXT file.

How does the bulk subtitle downloader work?

To download subtitles from multiple videos at once, paste each YouTube URL on a new line in the input box. When you click download, our tool will process all of them and bundle the resulting subtitle files into a single, convenient ZIP archive for you. See our Bulk Downloader page for more details.

What's the difference between SRT and TXT formats?

SRT files include timestamps and are used for displaying subtitles in video players. TXT files are plain text transcripts without any timing information, which are ideal for reading, quoting, or content analysis. We offer both so you can choose the format that best suits your needs.

Troubleshooting

Why does the tool say 'Subtitles not found'?

This message appears if the YouTube video either does not have any subtitles available (neither manual nor auto-generated) or if the subtitles have been disabled by the video owner. Our tool can only access publicly available captions.

The download isn't working. What should I do?

First, ensure the YouTube URL is correct and public. If it is, try clearing your browser cache or disabling any ad-blocking extensions, which can sometimes interfere with the download process. If the issue persists, please contact us for support.

Can I download subtitles for private or age-restricted videos?

No. Our tool can only access content that is publicly available on YouTube. It cannot process private videos, unlisted videos you don't have access to, or content that requires a login to view.
